## Start with your actual route

**Google Maps is extremely useful** for local transport and walking directions. For longer journeys, compare the specific legs in your itinerary before buying any national pass.

## Shinkansen and rail passes

The **Shinkansen is fast and comfortable**, but it can be expensive. After the nationwide JR Pass price increase in 2023, the pass is not automatically the best choice. Regional passes may suit a concentrated route better.

Pass prices, eligibility, sales periods and reservation rules change. Check the official operator pages before buying.

## Seat reservations

Major routes have frequent departures, but busy travel periods and particular seats can fill. On the Tokyo–Kyoto/Osaka line, travellers often choose the side with Mount Fuji views; confirm the direction and seat layout for your exact train.

## Night buses

**Night buses can save both travel time and a night of accommodation.** Willer is one operator to compare. Check the journey length, seat type and arrival point before choosing one.

## Local transport

Reloadable IC cards such as Suica and PASMO simplify many city journeys. Day passes can be useful when your plans involve repeated rides, but the best option depends on the city and route.

## Driving

Driving permissions depend on the licence-issuing country and current Japanese rules. Arrange any required international permit or official translation before the trip; do not assume an on-the-day translation is possible.
