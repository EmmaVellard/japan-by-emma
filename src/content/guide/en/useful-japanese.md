## Three writing systems

**Hiragana (ひらがな)**<br />
Used for Japanese words, grammatical particles and verb endings.

**Katakana (カタカナ)**<br />
Often used for foreign words, foreign names and onomatopoeia.

**Kanji (漢字)**<br />
Characters of Chinese origin used throughout everyday written Japanese.

You do not need to read all three to travel, but recognising a few signs and using camera translation can help.

## Polite basics

- **Konnichiwa** — Hello / good afternoon
- **Konbanwa** — Good evening
- **Arigatō gozaimasu** — Thank you very much
- **Sumimasen** — Excuse me / sorry
- **Onegai shimasu** — Please
- **Hai / iie** — Yes / no

## Useful questions

- **Eigo o hanasemasu ka?** — Do you speak English?
- **Kore wa ikura desu ka?** — How much is this?
- **Sumimasen, toire wa doko desu ka?** — Excuse me, where are the toilets?
- **Wi-Fi wa arimasu ka?** — Do you have Wi-Fi?

These are simplified phonetic notes, not a language course. Listening to a native pronunciation before travelling is worthwhile.
