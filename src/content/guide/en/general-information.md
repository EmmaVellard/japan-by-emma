## Konbinis are genuinely useful

Convenience stores such as **7-Eleven, FamilyMart and Lawson** are often open around the clock. Beyond snacks, coffee and meals that can be heated, many have ATMs, printers and toilets. Some offer additional services, but these vary by branch, so I treat a konbini as a very helpful option rather than a guarantee.

They became small landmarks in my days: somewhere to withdraw cash, buy water, print something or find an easy meal when the original plan had disappeared.

## Everyday safety

Japan felt **extremely safe to me**, including when travelling alone. Normal precautions still apply, especially with passports, money and late-night journeys.

That feeling of safety is comforting, but I would not let it make me careless. I keep a copy of important documents separately, store a backup card away from my wallet and save the accommodation address before going out.

## Carry a small rubbish bag

Public rubbish bins can be hard to find. You may carry packaging for a while, so keeping **a small reusable bag** in your day bag is surprisingly useful.

## Lockers

Coin lockers are common in stations, metro stations and some shopping areas. They are one of the details that make a hotel-change day much easier. Large spaces can fill, so I avoid assuming that the first locker bank will have room for a suitcase.

The Bounce app can help you find luggage storage, although it does not list every option. Save the station exit or take a photograph of the locker area before walking away.

## The small things in my day bag

I keep a little cash, a rubbish bag, a portable battery and the hotel address available offline. None of these is exciting enough for an itinerary, but together they remove many small stresses from the day.
