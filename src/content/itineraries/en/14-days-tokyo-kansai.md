## The journey I had in mind

This is the route I would choose for a **first encounter with Japan**. The first six days leave enough room to get lost in Tokyo without turning every morning into a race. Kamakura, Enoshima or Yokohama can become a day trip if the city begins to feel familiar; if not, Tokyo easily fills the time on its own.

Kyoto changes the rhythm. I wanted several nights there, followed by a deliberately open ending around Osaka, Nara, Kobe or Miyajima. The point is not to collect every name. It is to choose the places that suit your energy once the trip has started and leave a little room for the unexpected.

## Practical warning

The alternatives are intentionally flexible, but **Osaka, Nara and Miyajima are not interchangeable** in travel time. Choose the final combination before booking the last accommodation sequence.
