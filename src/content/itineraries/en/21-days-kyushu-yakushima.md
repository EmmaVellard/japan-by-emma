## Why I kept the southern route

This is the most adventurous route in my notes. It begins gently in Kyoto, passes through Kurashiki, Hiroshima and Miyajima, then keeps going south through Kyushu before giving Yakushima four full days. I wanted the island to feel like a destination in its own right, not a rushed excursion squeezed between ferries.

The appeal is the variety: **temple cities, the Inland Sea, hot-spring towns, volcanic landscapes and a forested island** in one journey. That variety also makes the route ambitious. There are several transport types, and weather can affect the connection to Yakushima.

Keep recovery time around the island journey and resist filling every Kyushu day. Removing one stop may make the remaining places feel more vivid—and make the whole trip feel more like a journal than a checklist.
