## Why this route stayed in my notes

I liked the idea of beginning with the energy of Tokyo and then watching the trip become quieter as the train moves north. Sendai, Yamadera, Aomori and Hirosaki give this route a very different texture from the classic Tokyo–Kyoto journey, before the final crossing to Hakodate and Sapporo.

It is a route for travellers who enjoy the movement between places as much as the destinations themselves. There are **more long transfers and fewer lazy mornings**, but also a stronger feeling of distance and changing landscape.

## Practical warning

My Hokkaido map is still growing, so treat its pins as incomplete and **verify every long-distance connection**, especially the final return from Sapporo.
