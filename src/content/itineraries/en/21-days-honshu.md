## What gives this route its character

This route draws one long line across Honshu instead of building the trip around a single region. After Tokyo, it jumps west to Hiroshima and then works its way back through Miyajima, Kurashiki and Kyoto before turning towards Kanazawa, Shirakawa-go and Takayama.

I kept it because it feels like a journey through several different Japans: **large cities, the Inland Sea, old merchant streets and mountain towns**. The transitions are part of the experience, but the pace is faster and the route combines high-speed trains with buses that run less frequently.

Careful booking matters more here than on the classic route. Check the Tokyo–Hiroshima leg and the mountain buses together before confirming hotels, then protect at least one quiet evening from the urge to add another stop.
