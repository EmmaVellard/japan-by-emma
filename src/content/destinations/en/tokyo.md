## How it fits into a journey

All three of my 14-day routes begin with **five or six days in Tokyo**. That may look generous on paper, but it gives the trip space to settle. It also leaves room for Kamakura, Enoshima or Yokohama without turning the capital into a two-day sprint.

For a first trip, I would not decide every day trip in advance. Keep one or two possible, then choose once you know whether you want a change of scale or another day inside Tokyo.

## A rhythm I would choose

- **Arrival day:** stay close to the hotel and make the first evening easy.
- **Full city days:** choose one broad area at a time instead of repeatedly crossing Tokyo.
- **One open day:** use it for a day trip, a place discovered on the map or a neighbourhood you want to revisit.
- **The final evening:** leave enough time to pack, forward luggage if useful and check the next morning’s station.

## Possible day trips

- Kamakura
- Enoshima
- Yokohama

These are options, not obligations. Tokyo can easily fill the entire stay on its own.

## Food and accommodation

I keep two different maps for a reason. **My Google My Maps is the broad notebook**: restaurants, cafes, hotels, museums, parks and places I am still considering. **My Mapstr is the personal shortlist** for restaurants and hotels I have actually tried.

When choosing accommodation, I look at the journeys I expect to repeat rather than searching for one supposedly perfect centre. Tokyo is too large for every area to be convenient for everything.

## Small details that make the city easier

Coin lockers can rescue a day between hotels, and convenience stores are useful for cash machines, simple meals and small travel emergencies. Save the name of the station exit you use: returning to the correct side of a large station can matter as much as remembering the station itself.

## Transport

Tokyo has two major international airports. **I prefer Haneda** because it is generally easier to reach from central Tokyo, but the best choice still depends on the fare, arrival time and hotel.

Before leaving for Kyoto, Osaka or northern Japan, check the exact departure station and train rather than relying on the route name alone. If you hope to see Mount Fuji from the Tokyo–Kyoto/Osaka train, confirm the current direction, train layout and seat side before reserving.
