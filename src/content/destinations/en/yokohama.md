## How I would organise the day

For a first visit, I would begin around **Sakuragichō and Minato Mirai**, then walk towards the Red Brick Warehouse, the waterfront and Yamashita Park. Chinatown can become the final part of the route rather than a separate journey across the city.

Sankeien Garden deserves its own block of time and sits away from the main harbour walk. I would include it only when the garden is a priority, not as another item squeezed into the same afternoon.

## A pace that feels right

- Choose the waterfront as the main thread for a first day.
- Walk in one direction instead of repeatedly returning to Yokohama Station.
- Keep time for the Red Brick Warehouse and harbour spaces without treating every building as a required stop.
- Stay into the evening if the illuminated skyline is part of what you want to see.

## Food and small pauses

Chinatown offers far more than a quick snack stop and can become crowded at popular hours. I would decide whether I want street food or a seated meal before arriving, then leave enough time to enjoy that choice without rushing back to the train.

## Transport

Several direct rail routes connect central Tokyo with Yokohama. Choose the arrival station according to the first part of the day: Yokohama Station for connections, Sakuragichō for Minato Mirai or Motomachi-Chūkagai for Chinatown and Yamashita Park.

The harbour area is walkable, but distances accumulate. Local buses can connect Minato Mirai, the Red Brick Warehouse, Yamashita and Chinatown when energy or weather changes.
