## How I would organise the day

For a first visit, I would choose one direction rather than zigzagging. One option is to begin at **Kita-Kamakura**, walk through one or two temple grounds, continue towards central Kamakura and finish around Tsurugaoka Hachimangū. Another is to begin in the centre, then take the Enoden to Hase for the Great Buddha, Hase-dera and the coast.

Trying to combine Kita-Kamakura, the eastern temples, central Kamakura, Hase and Enoshima in one day would change the visit into a transport exercise. I would keep Enoshima for a separate, longer coastal day.

## A pace that feels right

- Arrive early enough to enjoy at least one temple before the main day-trip crowds.
- Choose two or three important places and let the walks between them remain part of the day.
- Keep time for the Enoden rather than treating it only as transport.
- If the weather is clear, finish near Yuigahama or continue towards Enoshima without forcing another temple.

## Food and small pauses

Komachi-dōri is lively and convenient, but I also like the idea of leaving the busiest street for a quieter café or lunch near Hase. The broad Google map is where I keep possible addresses; Mapstr remains my list of places personally tested.

## Transport

Kamakura and Kita-Kamakura are reached by direct rail routes from central Tokyo, while the Enoden connects Kamakura with Hase and the coast. Check the exact route from your Tokyo neighbourhood on the day, because the best departure station changes with where you are staying.

Temple opening times, special access and seasonal conditions can vary. Verify the places that matter most before building the order of the day.
