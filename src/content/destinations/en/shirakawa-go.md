## How it fits into a journey

Shirakawa-go works naturally between Kanazawa and Takayama, but the bus timetable controls the day. A few hours are enough for the main village, one house interior, the open-air museum and a viewpoint when connections line up well.

Staying overnight changes the rhythm. The quiet before the first buses and after the last groups can become the strongest memory, especially when the weather makes the landscape feel enclosed.

## A simple route through the village

- Begin in Ogimachi and look at the whole landscape before choosing a house to enter.
- Cross Deai-bashi for the open-air museum if you want more context about gasshō construction and village life.
- Walk to the viewpoint only if conditions and time make it comfortable.
- Leave enough margin to return to the bus terminal without turning the final hour into a rush.

## Respecting the village

Stay on public paths, do not enter private property and remember that the fields and houses are part of residents’ daily lives. Winter conditions, snow-clearing work and seasonal events can change which routes are practical.

## Transport

There is no railway station in Shirakawa-go. Most journeys use reserved highway buses from Kanazawa, Takayama, Toyama or nearby hubs. Popular departures can sell out, so the outward and onward buses should be considered together before booking accommodation.

Check the official village and bus operator information shortly before travelling, especially in winter or during special illumination periods.
