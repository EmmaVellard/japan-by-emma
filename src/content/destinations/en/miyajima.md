## Day trip or overnight

A full day is enough for the shrine area, town streets, Daishō-in and part of Momijidani. Add Mount Misen only when the weather, ropeway operations and your energy make it a real choice rather than an obligation.

Staying overnight creates a quieter evening after the day visitors leave and an early morning before the shops reopen. If the route already includes Hiroshima, one night on the island can become a memorable pause rather than another excursion.

## A route I would choose

- Check the tide times, then decide whether to see the shrine first or return later for a different view.
- Walk beyond Omotesandō into Machiya-dōri and the smaller back streets.
- Continue to Daishō-in and Momijidani before deciding whether to go higher.
- Keep enough time for the ferry and the onward train rather than relying on the final possible connection.

## Food and the island

The busiest food street is part of the experience, but I would save time to sit down rather than eat every speciality while walking. Oysters and momiji manjū are closely associated with the area; opening days and availability still vary by shop and season.

## Transport and conditions

Miyajima is reached by ferry from the mainland, usually after a train or tram journey from Hiroshima. Different ferry operators and ticket combinations exist, so check the route that fits your rail plans.

Weather can affect mountain paths and ropeway service. Shrine hours, tide conditions and ferry timetables should be checked on official sites shortly before the visit.
